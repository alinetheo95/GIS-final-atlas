Urban Density

New York City has a poulation density of approximately 29,303 people per square mile, making it the densest city in the world.

Impervious Surfaces

In addition to being the most densely populated country in the world, more than half of New York City's surfaces are impervious.

Impervious surfaces increase flooding by preventing rainwater from soaking into the ground, they cause pollution by channeling run off into waterways, and they contribute to the urban heat island effect by absorbing and retaining heat.

63% of Manhattan's surfaces are impervious.
