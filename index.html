What would the New York City grid look like if it was more organic? A study of naturally occuring movement and growth through ecological history and morphogenesis growth algorithms.

Urban Density

In New York City, urban density can be defined by many factors. This study will address urban density in terms of impervious surface coverage and the history of the originaion of the grid.

The New York City grid originated from the Commissioner's Plan of 1811, which put in place the rectilinear grid plan. This was cheaper and more efficient for development and expansion, maximizing sellable rectagular lots. Although the grid may have been easier to navigate back then, it is not designed for shortest distance travels, natural movement patterns, or the pedestrian experience.

Impervious Surfaces

Today, New York City's land coverage is 73% impervious. Not only does this restrict organic pathways and desire lines, it also restricts movement and growth of ecological habitats. Lack of permeable surfaces is also causing harsh conditions due to climate change, such as urban heat island effect and increased flooding.
